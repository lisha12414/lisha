Smart Gloves For Sign Interpretation

The "Smart Gloves for Sign Interpretation" is an IoT-based project designed to assist speech-impaired individuals by translating hand gestures into text or speech in real time. Using a NodeMCU (ESP8266) microcontroller and five infrared sensors, the glove detects gestures, processes and converts the data, then transmits it wirelessly via Wi-Fi to an Android app for display. Developed with Arduino and Android Studio, the system is affordable, portable, user-friendly, and scalable. It finds applications in assistive tech, education, healthcare, smart homes, public services, and gaming. The project promotes social inclusion and multilingual communication, with a demo video accessible via QR code.
